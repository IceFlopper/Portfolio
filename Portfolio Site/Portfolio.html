Motivation
Why I chose Programming

Curiosity

I've always been interested in the way things around me work. Therefore, it's only natural that I gained an interest in the ways the software and hardware work that I interact with every single day. From entertainment to serious work, it's always been a part of my daily routine to use the software programmed and designed by developers.

Gaming

Gaming has always been a big influence in my life and an interest in becoming some kind of developer. I love the idea of shaping my own worlds and working together on a project with a team full of diverse and creative people. My mind has been filled with ideas ever since I've been a kid, trying to find the next cool thing, and I was always met with the same realization that nobody else seems to have the same idea and passion for quality these days. So, even though this may be a far-fetched dream from where I am now, it's only up from here.

People

I always strive to make others happy and satisfied as a worker. I go out of my way to make sure they get what they need and want, and try to do extra just in case to make sure they are satisfied. I do it because I genuinely enjoy it. Seeing the smile light up on someone's face when I find the item they need out of the racks in the grocery store or when I fix something that was broken has always driven me to be the best version of myself. I'm the same when it comes to software and hardware, driven by the same moral. I want to make sure people have a properly working bug-free experience and a good quality of life experience too. Having been an avid early access player for many games, I know what it's like to play buggy messes and interact with horrible UI, and I would love to participate in reducing these things.
